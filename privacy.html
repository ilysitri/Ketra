Privacy Policy

Effective 10 May 2026.

This Privacy Policy explains how Selah collects, uses, and protects your information. Selah is operated by Evie Maxine ROSE, a sole trader registered in New Zealand, trading as Selah (NZBN: 9429049441673).

We have written this policy to be readable. If anything is unclear, email skogsmuset@gmail.com.

Summary

Selah has no servers and no user accounts. Everything you do in the app — your journal traces, saved verses, streak, daily notes — is stored locally on your device only. The only external service the app contacts is RevenueCat, which processes subscription payments. We do not collect personal information, we do not track you across apps, and we do not sell your data.

1. What data Selah collects

Locally stored data

Selah stores the following on your device only, using your device's standard local storage (AsyncStorage). None of this data is sent to Selah or any third party other than what is described in Section 2:

  • Your name (optional, entered during onboarding).
  • Your onboarding responses (life season, motherhood, cycle-tracking preference).
  • Your daily notification time preference.
  • Verse traces — your one-line reflections attached to specific verses, with dates.
  • Saved verses — verse references you have bookmarked.
  • Your streak count and last-opened date.
  • Daily Bite verse and reflection question for the current day.
  • Guided Path progress — which path you are on and which days you have completed.
  • A flag indicating whether you have completed onboarding.
  • Your premium subscription status (cached locally, verified with RevenueCat).

What Selah does not collect

  • Your email address, phone number, or physical address.
  • Account credentials — Selah has no accounts.
  • Your location.
  • Your device's unique identifiers for advertising or tracking purposes.
  • Analytics or usage telemetry.
  • Crash reports beyond what your operating system provides to its developer.
  • The content of your verse traces or journal entries — these never leave your device.

2. Third-party services

RevenueCat

Selah uses RevenueCat to manage subscription purchases. When you subscribe to Selah Plus, RevenueCat processes the transaction through the Apple App Store and verifies your entitlement. RevenueCat receives a pseudonymous user identifier and your subscription status — it does not receive your name, email, or the content of anything you do in the app.

RevenueCat's privacy policy is available at revenuecat.com/privacy.

Apple App Store

If you download Selah through the Apple App Store, Apple collects its own data about your download and any in-app purchases. That collection is governed by Apple's privacy policy at apple.com/legal/privacy.

Push notifications

If you enable the daily reminder, Selah schedules a local notification on your device using Expo's notifications library. This notification is generated and delivered entirely on your device — no notification content is sent to any server.

4. Data retention

All locally stored data remains on your device until you delete it. You can delete it by:

  • Using the "Clear all data" option in Settings (visible in developer mode).
  • Uninstalling Selah — this removes all locally stored data.
  • Clearing the app's storage through your device's system settings.

RevenueCat retains purchase records per their own retention policy. You can request deletion of your RevenueCat data by contacting them directly.

5. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you have rights over your personal information under one or more of: the New Zealand Privacy Act 2020, the Australian Privacy Act 1988, the EU/UK General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA).

These rights generally include:

  • Access — to find out what data we hold about you.
  • Correction — to correct inaccurate data.
  • Deletion — to have your data deleted.
  • Portability — to receive your data in a machine-readable format.
  • Objection — to object to certain processing.
  • Withdrawal of consent — to withdraw any consent you have given.
  • Complaint — to lodge a complaint with your local privacy regulator.

Because Selah stores essentially everything on your own device and holds no server-side records, most of these rights are exercised directly on your device. To make a formal request or ask a question, email skogsmuset@gmail.com and we will respond within 30 days.

Privacy regulators you can contact:

  • New Zealand: Office of the Privacy Commissioner — privacy.org.nz
  • Australia: Office of the Australian Information Commissioner — oaic.gov.au
  • EU: Your national Data Protection Authority
  • California: California Attorney General's Office

6. Children

Selah is intended for users aged 17 and over, consistent with its App Store rating. We do not knowingly collect information from anyone under 17. If you believe a person under 17 has used Selah, contact us and we will take appropriate action.

7. Security

All communication with RevenueCat is transmitted over HTTPS. Locally stored data is protected by your device's standard storage security. Because Selah does not maintain its own servers, the app does not have a server-side data breach surface.

No system is completely secure. If a security incident occurs that affects your data, we will notify affected users and the relevant privacy regulator where required by law.

8. International transfers

When you make or restore a subscription, data is processed by RevenueCat, whose servers are located in the United States. RevenueCat maintains compliance frameworks for international data transfers. By using Selah Plus, you understand that subscription data may be processed in countries outside your own.

No other data leaves your device or crosses international borders.

9. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy as the app evolves. Material changes will be reflected in the "Effective" date at the top of this page. For significant changes, we will surface a notice within the app on next launch.
