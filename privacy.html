Privacy Policy

Effective 27 April 2026.

This Privacy Policy explains how Ketra collects, uses, and protects your information. Ketra is operated by Evie Maxine ROSE, a sole trader registered in New Zealand, trading as Ketra (NZBN: 9429049441673).

We have written this policy to be readable. If anything is unclear, email skogsmuset@gmail.com.

Summary. Ketra has no servers and no user accounts. The only data that leaves your device is the photos you take in the app, which are sent to Google's Gemini API for AI analysis. Saved recipes and free-tier counters are stored locally on your device only. We do not collect personal information, we do not track you across apps, and we do not sell your data.

1. What data Ketra collects

Photos you submit

When you take a photo in Ketra or select one from your device's photo library to scan, that image is transmitted to Google's Gemini API for analysis. Ketra itself does not store the photo. After Google returns the AI-generated response, the image is no longer used by the app.

Locally stored data

Ketra stores the following on your device only, using your device's standard local storage. None of this data is sent to Ketra or any third party (other than the AI analysis described above):

  • Saved recipes (recipe name, ingredients, method, source product name).
  • A counter tracking how many free recipe cards you have generated, for the purposes of the free-tier limit.
  • A flag indicating whether you have accepted the consent screen.

What Ketra does NOT collect

  • Your name, email, phone number, or address.
  • Account credentials (Ketra has no accounts).
  • Your location.
  • Your device's unique identifiers for advertising or tracking purposes.
  • Analytics or usage telemetry.
  • Crash reports beyond what your operating system provides to its developer.

2. Third-party services

Google Gemini API

Photos you scan are sent to the Gemini API, operated by Google LLC. Google processes the image to generate the keto verdict, nutrition information, and recipe text that Ketra displays back to you.

Google's handling of API requests is governed by their own terms and privacy policy, which you can read here:

  • Gemini API Additional Terms of Service
  • Google Privacy Policy

Per Google's published terms, content sent to the Gemini API on the paid tier is not used to train Google's models. On the free tier, content may be used to improve Google's services. Ketra's intent is to operate on the paid tier when distributed through app stores, but during development and early testing we may use the free tier. If the AI tier we use changes materially, this policy will be updated.

App stores

If you download Ketra through the Apple App Store or Google Play Store, those stores collect their own data about your download and any in-app purchases. That collection is governed by Apple's and Google's privacy policies respectively.

4. Data retention

Photos you submit to the Gemini API are handled per Google's retention policy for API requests, summarised in the Gemini documentation linked above. Ketra does not retain copies of your photos after the API response is received.

Locally stored data (saved recipes, counters, flags) remains on your device until you delete it. You can delete it by:

  • Deleting individual saved recipes from within the app.
  • Uninstalling Ketra (this removes all locally stored data).
  • Clearing the app's storage through your device's system settings.

5. Your rights

Depending on where you live, you have rights over your personal information under one or more of: the New Zealand Privacy Act 2020, the Australian Privacy Act 1988, the EU/UK General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), and the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA).

These rights generally include:

  • Access — to find out what data we hold about you.
  • Correction — to correct inaccurate data.
  • Deletion — to have your data deleted.
  • Portability — to receive your data in a machine-readable format.
  • Objection — to object to certain processing.
  • Withdrawal of consent — to withdraw any consent you have given.
  • Complaint — to lodge a complaint with your local privacy regulator.

Because Ketra holds essentially no personal data about you (no accounts, no server-side records), most of these rights are exercised by managing the data on your own device or by contacting Google directly about Gemini API requests. To make a request to us, email skogsmuset@gmail.com and we will respond within 30 days.

Privacy regulators you can contact if you have a concern:

  • New Zealand: Office of the Privacy Commissioner (privacy.org.nz)
  • Australia: Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (oaic.gov.au)
  • EU: Your national Data Protection Authority
  • California: California Attorney General's Office

6. Children

Ketra is intended for users aged 18 and over. We do not knowingly collect information from anyone under 18. If you believe a person under 18 has used Ketra, contact us and we will take appropriate action.

7. Security

Photos sent to the Gemini API are transmitted over HTTPS. Locally stored data is protected by your device's standard storage security. Because Ketra does not maintain servers, the app does not have a server-side data breach surface.

No system is completely secure. If a security incident occurs that affects your data, we will notify affected users and the relevant privacy regulator where required by law.

8. International transfers

When you scan a photo, the image is sent to Google's Gemini API. Google's servers are located in multiple countries including the United States. Google maintains compliance frameworks for international data transfers including under GDPR and the EU-US Data Privacy Framework. By using Ketra, you understand that scanned photos may be processed in countries outside your own.

9. Changes to this Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy. Material changes will be reflected in the "Effective" date at the top of this page. For significant changes, we will surface a notice within the app on next launch.
